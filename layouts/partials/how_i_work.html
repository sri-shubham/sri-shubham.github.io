How I Work: Engineering for the Long Term

When you hire me to build or audit your systems, you get software designed for longevity and clarity. My focus is on building resilient backends that solve immediate business problems while keeping future maintenance costs strictly under control.

Standard Library Purism

I prioritize simple, lightweight interfaces over complex dependency webs. Whenever possible, I rely on Go's idiomatic patterns and the standard library rather than pulling in heavy third-party packages. This results in faster execution, fewer security vulnerabilities, and code your team can maintain long after our engagement concludes.

Architecting for Predictability

The shift toward AI-driven workflows requires rigorous system design, not just basic API calls. Moving from fragile text outputs to structured schemas means building systems that are predictable and observable. I design robust architectures that keep your application logic transparent and your integrations stable.

The Pragmatic Business Mindset

Having built and managed independent SaaS tools, I understand that engineering is a means to a business end. I do not build complex features just for the sake of new technology. I evaluate technical decisions based on your return on investment, focusing on solutions that generate actual revenue and keep overhead low.

Clear Checkpoints & Transparent Communication

Reliable software requires reliable communication. We will establish clear validation checkpoints throughout the software development life cycle. This ensures the architecture remains aligned with your business goals and prevents costly pivots later in the process.
